Tatiana Robinson was born and raised in Southeast, Washington DC and is the second of four children. She has been a DC Public Schools student during her entire school career attending Randle Highlands Elementary School, Jefferson Middle School, and Ballou High School. Tatiana served in numerous leadership roles, was involved in various school clubs and sports such as golf, flag football, softball, bowling, and track and field. She was also dually-enrolled at Howard University, served as Ballou’s Representative on the DCPS Chancellor’s Student Cabinet, and Vice President of Ballou’s Book Club. She also served as a member of Mayor Bowser’s Chancellor Search Committee, a group of DC stakeholders selected to aid Mayor Muriel Bowser in the selection of the DC Public Schools Chancellor. Additionally, she was involved in the Ballou Ladies of Excellence Mentoring Programs and Minds Matter DC, a Saturday SAT Prep and mentoring program, to help prepare her for the future. Tatiana continuously looked for opportunities to advocate not only for herself, but most importantly for her peers to ensure that they felt prepared for their future and had their voices heard by education policy makers. Tatiana served two terms as the Student Representatives on the DC State Board of Education from 2017-2019. As the Student Representative, she served as the liaison and voice for the student population in DC. This role provided Tatiana with the opportunity to work with District policy makers as it relates to education and public safety, build upon her leadership and public speaking skills, and provided more opportunities for advocacy including - serving as the CoChair of the Student Advisory Committee and creating a peer-to-peer mentoring program for students across the city.

Now, Tatiana attends the University of Miami, majoring in Interactive Media and minoring in Game Design, Computer Science, and Mathematics. You can find more about her future plans in the Future Goals tab of this website.
